Koshari

Description

Koshari is Egypt's national dish and a beloved street food, celebrated for its rich combination of flavors and textures. It’s a hearty vegetarian dish made with layers of lentils, rice, chickpeas, and pasta, topped with a spicy tomato sauce, crispy fried onions, and a tangy garlic vinegar dressing. This wholesome dish is an inexpensive and filling meal that represents the heart of Egyptian cuisine, combining Indian, Italian, and Middle Eastern culinary influences. Koshari is a true comfort food that’s enjoyed by people of all ages, offering a burst of bold and savory flavors in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white rice
  • 1 cup brown lentils
  • 1 cup small pasta (e.g., elbow macaroni)
  • 1 cup canned chickpeas (rinsed and drained)
  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for spice)
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • Water (as needed for boiling and cooking)

Steps

  1. Cook the rice:
    • Wash the rice thoroughly until the water runs clear.
    • Cook the rice in a pot with water and a pinch of salt according to package instructions.
  2. Cook the lentils:
    • Wash the lentils and boil them in a pot with water until tender (about 20-25 minutes).
    • Drain the lentils and set aside.
  3. Cook the pasta:
    • Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente (firm to the bite).
    • Drain the pasta and toss with a little oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Prepare the crispy onions:
    • Heat the vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
    • Fry the sliced onions until golden brown and crispy, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
    • Remove the onions from the oil and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  5. Prepare the tomato sauce:
    • Heat a small amount of oil in a saucepan over medium heat.
    • Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
    • Stir in the tomato sauce, vinegar, cumin, chili powder (if using), salt, and pepper.
    • Simmer the sauce for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Set aside.
  6. Assemble the dish:
    • In a serving dish, layer the cooked rice at the bottom.
    • Add a layer of lentils on top of the rice.
    • Add the cooked pasta and chickpeas as additional layers.
    • Pour the tomato sauce evenly over the top.
    • Garnish with the crispy fried onions.
  7. Serve and enjoy your delicious Egyptian Koshari!
