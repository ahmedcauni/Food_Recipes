Molokkhia

Description

Molokkhia is a beloved dish in Egyptian cuisine, made from finely chopped jute leaves that create a flavorful, slightly viscous soup. Often prepared with a rich broth, garlic, and coriander, Molokkhia is served with rice or bread and sometimes paired with chicken, beef, or rabbit. This traditional dish is highly nutritious and has been a staple of Egyptian households for generations, symbolizing warmth and comfort. Its unique texture and bold flavors make it an iconic meal that brings families together around the dinner table.

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch fresh Molokkhia leaves (or 400g frozen Molokkhia)
  • 4 cups chicken, beef, or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons butter or ghee
  • 4-5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • Cooked white rice or flatbread (for serving)
  • 1 whole chicken, beef pieces, or rabbit (optional, for serving)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Molokkhia leaves:
    • If using fresh Molokkhia, wash the leaves thoroughly, remove the stems, and finely chop the leaves.
    • If using frozen Molokkhia, allow it to thaw completely before cooking.
  2. Prepare the broth:
    • In a large pot, bring the broth to a simmer.
    • If using chicken, beef, or rabbit, cook the meat in the broth until tender, then remove and set aside.
  3. Cook the garlic and coriander:
    • In a small pan, melt the butter or ghee over medium heat.
    • Add the minced garlic and saut√© until golden brown and fragrant.
    • Stir in the ground coriander and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add the Molokkhia to the broth:
    • Stir the garlic-coriander mixture into the simmering broth.
    • Gradually add the chopped or thawed Molokkhia leaves while stirring.
    • Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
    • Simmer gently for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the Molokkhia is fully cooked and slightly thickened.
  5. Serve the dish:
    • Place cooked white rice or flatbread on a serving plate or bowl.
    • Ladle the hot Molokkhia over the rice or serve it alongside the bread.
    • Add the cooked chicken, beef, or rabbit to the plate for a heartier meal.
  6. Enjoy your Molokkhia:
    • Serve immediately while hot, and enjoy this traditional and nutritious Egyptian dish.
